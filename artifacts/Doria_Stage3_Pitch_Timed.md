# Doria Stage 3 Pitch

Aligned to the Stage 3 finals structure and the sample pitch deck flow.

## 0:00-1:00 Introduction + National Vision

Good afternoon distinguished judges.

I am Alex Kyalo, founder of Doria.

Today, I present Doria, an AI-powered incident intelligence platform that helps government institutions move from fragmented incident reporting to coordinated, data-driven response.

I envision a Kenya where public institutions can detect, understand, and respond to incidents in real time through one unified operational intelligence layer.

Doria is not just digitizing reports. It is building intelligent incident coordination infrastructure for the nation.

## 1:00-3:00 National Security Context / Strategic Gap

As Kenya continues to modernize public service and critical operations, one strategic gap remains clear: incident information is still fragmented across institutions, facilities, and teams.

This creates three national risks: slow response, limited cross-agency visibility, and weak awareness of emerging hotspots and repeat threats.

Today, incidents are often logged in isolated systems and followed up manually. That means decision-makers see only fragments of the operational picture.

A facility may detect a threat. A field team may log an update. Another institution may also be affected. But without shared intelligence, response becomes delayed and reactive.

In a national security environment, fragmented incident response is a resilience gap.

## 3:00-4:30 Cross-Sectoral Resilience

Doria strengthens multiple sectors at the same time.

In public safety, it improves incident capture and follow-up. In energy and utilities, it improves visibility around threats and disruptions. In transport and logistics, it helps identify patterns and operational hotspots. In public administration, it creates accountable and auditable response workflows.

Instead of isolated operations, Doria creates a shared intelligence workflow built around facilities, incidents, and actionable AI insights.

This is cross-sector resilience by design.

## 4:30-6:00 National Sovereignty & Control

Our system is built with sovereignty and institutional control in mind.

Operational data can remain under government-controlled infrastructure. The platform supports controlled deployment for sensitive environments. Its architecture is modular, maintainable, and designed for local ownership.

This allows institutions to retain control over their data, workflows, and long-term operational evolution.

## 6:00-9:00 Demo

Let me walk you through a real operational scenario.

An incident is reported at a facility. The system captures the incident type, geolocation, institution context, and follow-up ownership.

Immediately after that, Doria applies AI to the operational dataset and returns structured intelligence.

It generates a summary. It classifies risk level. It highlights top patterns. It identifies facility hotspots. It surfaces follow-up gaps. And it recommends priority actions for human review.

This means leaders no longer have to read through scattered records to understand what is happening. They move from raw incident data to operational insight in minutes.

Doria also includes area intelligence.

A decision-maker can select a geographic area and instantly analyze the facilities and incidents inside that corridor. The system then returns a structured operational view of that zone, including observed risks and recommended deployment posture.

Previously, this would require multiple manual checks and delayed coordination. With Doria, the process becomes faster, clearer, and more actionable.

Every update is tied to ownership. Every follow-up is traceable. Every insight is structured for review.

## 9:00-11:00 Scalability & Speed to Impact

Doria is built to scale in stages.

Phase 1 is pilot deployment with incident capture, follow-up workflows, and AI summaries in selected institutions or counties. Phase 2 expands facility coverage and cross-agency visibility. Phase 3 enables broader national coordination, stronger geospatial intelligence, and predictive operational alerts.

To scale, Doria integrates into existing workflows rather than forcing institutions to replace everything at once.

This allows rapid impact with controlled implementation risk.

## 11:00-12:30 Trust, Ethics & Governance

Trust, accountability, and governance are built into the platform.

Doria includes role-based access control, scoped visibility, audit trails, and structured operational ownership. Its AI outputs are designed to be understandable and reviewable, not black-box decisions. The system is privacy-aware and built for institutional accountability.

In public operations, intelligence must be fast, secure, auditable, and governable.

## 12:30-13:30 Economic & Operational ROI

Operationally, Doria reduces the cost of fragmented response.

It shortens the time spent reconciling scattered reports. It improves allocation of attention and resources. It helps institutions identify hotspots earlier. And it increases accountability through visible follow-up ownership and traceable action history.

The cost of inaction is delay, duplication, and weak coordination. The benefit of Doria is faster triage, stronger visibility, and better institutional response.
